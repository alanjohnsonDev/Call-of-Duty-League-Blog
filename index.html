Results of Major II Tournament

Alan Johnson, April 4, 2022

Congratulations to the Los Angeles Guerrillas for an amazing comeback performance in the Major II Tournament. The Guerrillas were in an uphill battle throughout the entire Tournament. Facing the Boston Breach, to go down 3-0 in that matchup in the winners bracket, but to make an outstanding comeback throughout the rest of the tournament. Not only did they have to face a red hot London Royal Ravens team in the Elimination bracket, but to flip the script and go up 3-0 on the Royal Ravens was amazing to watch.

The Seattle Surge gave the Guerrillas a fight for their money, by taking them to a map 5 on Bocage S&D, but the Guerillas stood their ground and was able to squeak out a win against Seattle. In Round 4, the Guerrillas faced a tough Florida Mutineers team that had Los Angeles on the ropes in the beginning. The Mutineers were coming in hot with wins over the LA Theives and Optic Texas. The Guerrillas came back in the map 4 Hardpoint - Berlin, and closed out a Round 11 victory on S&D Desert Siege.

The Guerrillas have to play the Boston Breach, the team that kicked them out of the winners bracket, in the Elimination finals to push towards the Major Finals versus Atlanta FaZe. Los Angeles comes out swinging gaining a 2-0 map lead over the Boston Breach, winning the first Hardpoint - Berlin 250-239 and S&D - Bocage 6-1. The Breach do gain the next two maps on Control - Tuscan 3-2 and Hardpoint - Tuscan 250-221. Was the Breach going to be the team that kicks the Los Angeles Guerrillas out of their Major Finals hopes like they kicked them out of the Winners Bracket? NOPE! Guerrillas stand their ground on an S&D - Berlin and win against the Breach 6-2. Guerrillas make it to the Major Finals vs Atlanta FaZe.

Now Atlanta FaZe has been smoking the competition to get to the Major Finals. FaZe blew past the New York Subliners 3-0, the Seattle Surge 3-2, and the Boston Breach 3-0 in the Winners Finals. So, lets just say this is a tall task for the Los Angeles Guerrillas, even though they have been great themselves pushing through the eliminations bracket.

The stage is set for the Major Finals the crowd is going wild, with most routing for FaZe, but some routing for the underdog, Guerrillas. Remember in the Major Finals its a best of 9 maps, not 5. FaZe comes out hot beating the Guerrillas in a Hardpoint - Gavutu 250-135. Los Angeles Guerrillas take that hardpoint on the chin, but get back up and take maps 2 and 3, S&D - Bocage 6-4 and Control - Gavutu 3-2. Teams take a breather to chat with their coaches to see if they can fix up any communication before going into Map 4.

Map 4 Hardpoint - Tuscan: Guerrillas take a close win 250-227, which lead them into a convincing victory on Map 5 S&D - Desert Siege 6-1! Guerrillas are feeling really good at this moment, being up 4-1 against FaZe in the Finals.

Map 5 Control - Tuscan: FaZe makes a great stand at the end of the map to take it 3-2. FaZe is finally getting some momentum going into Map 6, but is it little to late.

Map 6 S&D - Berlin: Guerrillas love this map, it is their "bread and butter". Atlanta put up a great fight to take it to a round 10, but Guerrillas take the map and the match, beating FaZe 6-4 in Berlin - S&D.

Congratulations to the Los Angeles Guerrillas on completing their cinderella story. I forgot to tell you, they didn't have Gunless, they had to bring in a substitute "Spart". Spart really impressed me and the codcasters, I think he could be a starter in this league.

This was another amazing weekend of the Call of Duty League. Major III will start May 13th with Qualifiers. Search Call of Duty League on YouTube to get the latest matches and information.

Whats Next?

Alan Johnson, April 7, 2022
